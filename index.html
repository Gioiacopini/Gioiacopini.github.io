My Research





I am currently enrolled in the University of Sheffield’s 4 year PhD programme with integrated MSc in data analytics and society.

This degree programme is linked to the ESRC Centre for Doctoral Training (CDT) in Data Analytics and Society which is a collaborative initiative funded by the ESRC, with considerable financial input from four universities (University of Manchester, University of Leeds, University of Liverpool and University of Sheffield ) plus co-funding, data and other resources from non-academic partners.

The combined financial contributions that fund the CDT is valued at over £6m. This significant investment has been catalysed by the concerns of employers and the funding councils about the shortage of skills among social science graduates relating to the analysis and management of new forms of data.

In the specifics, I was selected to carry out the project investigating the Strategic Role of Data Analytics in Commercial Marketing .

Lead Supervisor: Dr. Todd Hartmann
Co-Supervisor: Prof. Gwilym Pryce
External Partner: Linney Group

The aim of this project is to develop cutting-edge marketing analysis applied to data on sales, store location and products from a variety of Linney clients. Our plan is to develop a series of tools and models using “network analysis”.

Such methods allow us to look at the relationship between many “nodes”. These nodes could be retail outlets, products or individual consumers.
